Drive Less Than 50 Miles A Day? See If You Qualify For Lower Rates

Many US drivers don't realize that driving fewer miles can mean lower auto insurance premiums.

If your daily drive is short — to the store, to work nearby, or simply less than it used to be — you may be paying for miles you don't actually drive.

Several US insurers now offer low-mileage or pay-per-mile options for drivers who log under 50 miles a day on average. Monthly rates for qualifying drivers start around $30 a month.

Less time on the road usually means lower risk — and many insurers factor that into your premium when you compare.
