Drive Less Than 50 Miles A Day? See If You Qualify For Lower Rates

Many drivers don't realize how much daily mileage affects their auto insurance premium.

Auto insurance is priced on risk, and risk goes up with miles. If you drive less than 50 miles a day, you may qualify for low-mileage or pay-per-mile rates that simply aren't shown to high-mileage drivers.

The threshold matters — drivers logging under 30 miles/day typically see the steepest savings.

Why low-mileage drivers pay less

Fewer miles means fewer chances of being involved in a claim. Several US insurers now offer dedicated low-mileage tiers — some only visible if you ask, others built into pay-per-mile programs (you pay a small base + per-mile usage).

If you work from home, drive short commutes, or share a car with a partner, you're likely a strong candidate for a low-mileage tier. Rates from $30/mo for qualifying drivers.
