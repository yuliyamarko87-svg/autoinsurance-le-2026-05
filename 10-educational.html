1. How US auto insurance rates are priced

Three factors carry the most weight: state (each has different minimums and claims patterns), driving history (clean records get lower rates), and vehicle profile (newer/safer cars often pay less for collision). Almost everything else — credit score, mileage, marital status — adjusts those base lines up or down.

2. Why rates vary so much between states

The state you live in often matters more than your driving record. Florida averages roughly 80% higher than Ohio for the same driver profile. Reasons include claims frequency, weather risks, and population density.

If you've moved recently, your current rate may not reflect your new state. Comparing again post-move is usually worth it.

3. The 4 discounts most drivers don't ask about

Low-mileage discount. If you drive under 50 miles a day, ask. Some carriers offer dedicated tiers — others fold it into pay-per-mile programs.
Multi-car bundle. Combining 2+ vehicles on one policy typically lowers per-car cost by 10-25%.
Home + auto bundle. Adding homeowner's coverage often saves another 10-20% on auto.
Safe-driver / good-student programs. Free monitoring apps can shave 10-30% off if your driving is clean.
