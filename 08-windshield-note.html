I Came Out Of The Store And Saw This Note On My Windshield

It said five words. They saved me $84 a month.

[Hero: folded handwritten note under windshield wiper · crea-brief 08-A]

It was a Tuesday afternoon. I came out of the grocery store, pushed the cart toward my car, and saw a small folded piece of paper tucked under my windshield wiper.

I figured it was a parking ticket or one of those neighborhood flyers. It wasn't.

"Check your car insurance. Saved me $84/mo." — the entire note. No name, no number.

So I checked

I went home and did exactly that. Pulled up a comparison tool, entered my ZIP, and within minutes I had several quotes side by side. My existing policy was $122/mo. The new quote with comparable coverage? $36/mo.

That's $86 a month — almost exactly what the note said.

What I never figured out

I never found out who left the note. The parking lot was busy, the wiper was on the driver side, and the handwriting wasn't familiar. My best guess is a neighbor or coworker who'd just switched their own insurance and was feeling evangelical about it.

Three months later I'm $84/mo better off. If you've been on the same auto policy for more than a year or two, it might be worth checking too — for me it took about 30 seconds to enter the ZIP and another 5 minutes to read the quotes.
