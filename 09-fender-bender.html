His Insurance Doubled After A Fender-Bender — Here's How He Brought It Down

A minor parking-lot bump shouldn't double a premium. But it can. Here's what one US driver did about it.

Marcus, a 42-year-old US driver, had a clean record for years. Then last spring, a tiny parking-lot bump — almost no damage, no injuries — went through his insurance. His next renewal jumped from $94/mo to $189/mo.

"A small accident doesn't mean every insurer surcharges the same way. Some price the bump in heavily; some barely. The comparison made that obvious." — paid actor portrayal. Individual results vary.

Why this works

Insurance carriers use different underwriting models. The same minor incident can produce a $95 surcharge at one carrier and a $5 surcharge at another. You're not stuck with the carrier that surcharged you the most — and comparison is the easiest way to find that out.
